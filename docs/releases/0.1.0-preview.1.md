# ApiaryLens 0.1.0 Preview 1

## Release status

ApiaryLens Preview 1 is the current **Public Preview**. It is intended for
families, hobbyists, mentors, and operators who want to evaluate the product and
help shape what comes next. It is not GA or a stable release. Features, workflows,
and interfaces may change, and updates may arrive frequently.

## What is available

- A safe, resettable demonstration at [ApiaryLens.app](https://apiarylens.app).
- The responsive, installable, offline-capable PWA for apiaries, hives, queens,
  equipment, inspections, photos, health and mite records, feeding, treatments,
  harvests, follow-ups, and family workflows.
- Built-in accounts, owner/beekeeper/viewer roles, invitations, private media,
  synchronization, export, backup, restore, and conflict recovery.
- A Cloudflare-native deployment for Workers, D1, R2, and the optional web frontend
  in an account controlled by the operator.
- A portable Docker Compose deployment for a local Linux computer, personally
  controlled hardware, or a Linux VM in Azure, AWS, Google Cloud, or another
  suitable provider.
- Public user, operator, security, architecture, deployment, and developer
  documentation.

Scout Bee is a separately versioned deployment bootloader that is coming soon. The
standalone Windows application is also future work. Neither is part of the current
Preview 1 end-user download offering.

## Preview safety

The public demo uses sample records. Do not enter private hive, identity, or family
data into it. Use a deployment you control for real records.

Keep a current backup and export before every update or migration. Do not treat
Preview 1 as the sole copy of irreplaceable hive records or media. Review the
[update and recovery lifecycle](../architecture/versioning-release-and-update-lifecycle.md)
before changing a deployment.

Preview 1 does not promise permanent free provider allowances, uninterrupted
availability, or compatibility across every future Preview update.

## Feedback and support

Report reproducible product issues with the release, deployment profile, browser or
device, and sanitized diagnostics where possible. Never include passwords, tokens,
private hive locations, or irreplaceable media in an issue. Security and data-loss
risks should follow the [security policy](../../SECURITY.md).

See the [changelog](changelog.md), [roadmap](../roadmap/roadmap.md), and
[verification instructions](verification.md) for current project information.
